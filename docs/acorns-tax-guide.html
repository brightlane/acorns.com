Capital Gains Tax on Acorns

When Acorns rebalances your portfolio (selling some ETFs to buy others), those sales generate capital gains or losses that appear on your 1099-B. You must report these on your federal tax return.

IRA Tax Advantages — Why Acorns Later Matters

Roth IRA: Contributions are after-tax. Growth and qualifying withdrawals are 100% tax-free. No annual 1099 for growth. Ideal for long-term retirement savings.

Traditional IRA: Contributions may be tax-deductible. Growth is tax-deferred. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income. Generates a 1099-R when you withdraw.

How to Minimize Taxes on Acorns

  • Use Acorns Later (IRA) for maximum tax advantage — growth is tax-free (Roth) or tax-deferred (Traditional)
  • Don't withdraw from taxable accounts early — each withdrawal may trigger capital gains taxes
  • Hold investments long-term — long-term capital gains rates are significantly lower than short-term
  • Don't ignore your 1099 — even small amounts from rebalancing must be reported to avoid IRS notices
Frequently Asked Questions
Does Acorns send tax forms?
Yes — Acorns sends a 1099 tax form by February 15 each year for taxable accounts (Acorns Invest) with reportable activity. IRA accounts (Acorns Later) don't generate annual 1099s but do require reporting of withdrawals.
What taxes do I pay on Acorns investments?
You pay capital gains tax when you sell investments for a profit, and income tax on dividends received. Investments held over 1 year qualify for the lower long-term capital gains rate (0%, 15%, or 20% depending on income).
Is the Acorns $3/month fee tax deductible?
Under current US tax law (post-2017), investment advisory fees paid for taxable accounts are no longer deductible for most individuals. Consult a tax professional for advice specific to your situation.
How does Acorns handle taxes on Round-Ups?
The Round-Up amounts themselves are not taxable when they're invested — you're just moving your own money. Taxes apply when the investments are sold for a gain, or when dividends are distributed.
