UTMA vs UGMA — What's the Difference?

UGMA (Uniform Gifts to Minors Act): Covers financial assets — stocks, bonds, mutual funds, ETFs. Available in all states.

UTMA (Uniform Transfers to Minors Act): Covers a broader range including real estate and other property. Available in most states. Acorns Early uses UTMA where available.

Both work the same way for investing: you manage as custodian, child gets control at 18–21 depending on state law.

Tax Considerations for Custodial Accounts

Custodial accounts don't have the tax advantages of IRAs. Investment earnings are subject to the "Kiddie Tax" — up to $2,300/year in investment income is taxed at the child's rate (usually 0–10%), above that it's taxed at the parent's rate.

Acorns Early is a custodial investment account (UTMA/UGMA) for minors. Parents or guardians open and manage the account on behalf of a child. The child takes control of the account when they reach the age of majority (18 or 21 depending on state).
